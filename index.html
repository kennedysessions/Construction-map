New York City construction fatalities and injuries decline in 2024

City data shows fewer construction workers died this year than in nearly ten years with more city-planned construction to come.

By: Kennedy Sessions, Dec. 22, 2024

Five construction workers died on the job in New York City in 2024 and 482 were injured. According to data from the NYC Department of Buildings, the numbers show a drop from 2023 when seven construction workers died and 692 were injured.

This marks New York City's lowest fatality and injury rate since 2015.

As construction activity remains consistent post-pandemic, levels are expected to increase following the city council's approval of Mayor Eric Adams's City of Yes Housing plan, which aims to build 80,000 housing units over the next 15 years.

"There is nothing more important than safety. The annual DOB Construction Safety Report shows the progress the city has made in reducing the number of construction-related fatalities to the lowest number in a decade. With that said, there is still more work to be done as workplace injuries are still a concern," ABC Empire State Chapter President Brian Sampson said in a press release.
